Who Are Ivan And Daniela Bencic? Meet Belinda Bencic Parents: Relationship &amp; Family

Belinda Bencic is a professional tennis player from Switzerland. She is the daughter of Ivan and Daniela Bencic. The athlete’s father and mother had a significant part in her unrivaled and incredible career. Belinda’s father, Ivan, has even dedicated his days to teaching and converting her into one of the top female tennis players in the world.

Furthermore, the athlete’s biological mother’s contribution to her upbringing is equal to her father’s. Her youth was spent in Flawil, Switzerland, with her parents and only brother, Brian Bencic. Belinda’s career has included a successful adventure. She made history by beginning to play tennis at the age of two and became the first Swiss athlete to win a gold medal in Women’s Singles at the 2020 Tokyo Olympics.

Furthermore, on February 17, 2020, the tennis player was rated “Number 4” As of right now, this is her career-high ranking. Not only has Belinda left her mark on tennis history, but she has also amassed a sizable fortune from her job. Whether it was the prize money or her typical wage, it all added up to her outstanding net worth of $6 million as of 2023.

Belinda Bencic Parents: Ivan And Daniela Bencic

Belinda was born on March 10, 1997, in Flawil, Switzerland to her father, Ivan, and mother, Daniela. Ivan, her father, was a professional hockey player in the Swiss National League A and National League B. Ivan then worked as an Insurance Broker before becoming a full-time coach. Belinda’s mother, Daniela, on the other hand, used to be a high-level handball player.

Because both of Belinda’s parents are athletes, it seemed inevitable that she would pursue an athletic career as well. Brian, her younger brother, was also a professional tennis player who participated on the ATP Tour and served as a coach from March 2020 until June 2021. However, he settled down to be the Insurance and Pension Advisor at Generali in the middle of 2021.

Ivan Bencic has always been a fan of Belinda Bencic

Ivan, Belinda’s father, has had a significant influence on her life. Her father started her tennis when she was two years old. Ivan aspired for her to be as proficient as Martina Hingis, the world’s number-one tennis player at the time. As a result, he arranged for her to attend a training session with Hingis’s mother and the instructor, Melanie Molitor. Belinda won the two junior Grand Slam Single championships at Wimbledon and the French Open at the age of 16 because of consistent and persistent work.

She eventually started her professional career in the 2014 US Open, when she became the second-youngest quarterfinalist after Martini Hingis in 1997. Later in her career, Belinda succeeded in the sport, setting several records, including gold (singles) and silver (doubles) in the 2020 Olympics. Belinda would not have been able to achieve such success without the help of her father. Not to mention that Ivan has been her daughter’s coach for many years.
