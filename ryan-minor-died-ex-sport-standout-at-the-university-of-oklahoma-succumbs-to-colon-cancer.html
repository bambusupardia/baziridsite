Ryan Minor Died, Ex-Sport Standout at the University of Oklahoma, Succumbs to Colon Cancer

Publish date: 2024-08-10

Ryan Minor, a former two-sport standout at the University of Oklahoma, has tragically passed away following his battle with stage 4 colon cancer. Minor was 49 years old and left an indelible mark on the sports world.

A Stellar Sports Career

Minor was an All-American in basketball and a pitcher and first baseman for the university’s baseball team. His twin brother, Damon, announced his passing on social media.

Battle with Colon Cancer

The news of Ryan’s cancer diagnosis was made public earlier this year when a GoFundMe page was set up to support his medical expenses. Despite the diagnosis, Minor remained resilient throughout his battle with the disease.

About Ryan Minor

Ryan Minor was more than just an athlete; he was a dedicated family man and a beloved figure in his community. His passing leaves a void in the hearts of many who knew him personally and those who admired his accomplishments from afar.

Leaving a Legacy

Although Ryan Minor is no longer with us, his legacy lives on. His impact on the sports world, especially at the University of Oklahoma, will be remembered for years to come.

As we mourn the loss of Ryan Minor, we also celebrate his life and the contributions he made during his remarkable career. Our thoughts and condolences go out to his family, friends, and the entire sports community during this difficult time.
